On our pages, On our skin.

-----------Lauren Wheeler,

in our preoccupations with INK.

How it connects to our art,
how it connects to our queerness,
how being tattooed has affected us: we will tell the story behind our
tattoos.

We as writers -- more than any other artists-- are preoccupied with ink.
After all, it's how we make our art. Painters have paint, sculptors
have their menagerie of clay and metal, but writers--
only ink and page.

Unless of course, they choose to have ink, page and skin.
